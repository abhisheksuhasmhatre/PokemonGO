Niantic introduced events months after releasing the game to help trainers gain advanatage in all forms including candies, XP rewards and much more.

Halloween Event

October 26 - November 1

From October 26th to November 1st, there were increased
spawn rates of certain "spooky" Pokemon,
including other bonuses.

The following Pokemon showed up at a much higher
rate than normal even if these Pokemon don't normally
appear in your areas:
Zubat, Golbat, Cubone, Marowak, Gastly, Haunter,
Grengar, Drowzee and Hypno.

Also included was the temporary ability to gain Pokemon
Candy at increased rates.
This includes the following candy-obtaining methods:

  • Catching a Pokemon (6 Candies each)
  • Transferring Pokemon (2 candies each)
  • Walking a Buddy Pokemon (4x normal rate)
  • Hatching a Pokemon (2x normal candies)

Thanksgiving Event

November 23 - November 30

The second Pokemon Go event ever was announced on Niantic's
Pokemon Go site on November 21, 2016.
From November 23rd at 00:00 UTC and going through
November 30th 00:00 UTC, trainers received double
the amount of XP and Stardust when completing in-game actions

Christmas Event

December 25 - January 3

PokéStops will awarded one single-use Incubator
each day after first spin. There were greater
chances of finding Eggs that hatch Togepi, Pichu,
and several other recently found baby Pokémon.
Special Pikachu wearing festive hats were found
everywhere!

Starter Event

December 30 - January 8

In addition, from the afternoon of December 30, 2016, through the afternoon of January 8, 2017 (PST),
the first partner Pokémon and their Evolutions, originally discovered in the Kanto region, are more likely to be encountered. This means a greater chance to catch Bulbasaur, Ivysaur, and Venusaur, as well as Charmander, Charmeleon, and Charizard, plus Squirtle, Wartortle, and Blastoise! As a way to increase your chance to
encounter one of these Pokémon, Lure Modules will also last for 60 minutes instead of 30 minutes.
